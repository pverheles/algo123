Definition

A randomized algorithm uses random choices during its execution. We study its expected runtime or error probability over the internal randomness.

Types

Las Vegas

Always correct; runtime is random (e.g., randomized quicksort).

Monte Carlo

Bounded runtime; answers may err with small probability (e.g., primality tests).
